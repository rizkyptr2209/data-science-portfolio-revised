Data Science Final Project | Dibimbing Data Science Bootcamp (2025)
 View on GitHub
 Download Deck

In today's competitive e-commerce space, understanding customer behavior is critical for retention and profitability. This project uses real-world transactional data from the Olist marketplace in Brazil to segment customers using RFML (Recency, Frequency, Monetary, Loyalty) and predicted their total sales contribution using Random Forest.

The model achieved R² = 0.727 and MAPE = 17%, enabling data-driven prioritization of customer segments by predicting sales contribution with 72% accuracy and segmenting users into five actionable profiles.

Methodology

Framework

This project follows the CRISP-DM framework, ensuring a structured and business-driven data science workflow. The process begins with understanding business objectives related to customer behavior and revenue optimization, followed by data understanding and preparation to address missing values, outliers, and feature engineering. Modeling and evaluation stages focus on aligning technical performance with business relevance, while the final deployment and insights phases translate analytical results into clear recommendations for decision-making.

Visualization

Data visualizations play a critical role in bridging analysis and business communication. Tools such as Tableau and Python-based plotting libraries are used to present model performance, customer distributions, and segmentation patterns in a clear and intuitive manner. These visualizations are designed not only to explore data internally but also to support stakeholder understanding through dashboards that emphasize trends, comparisons, and actionable insights.

RFML Segmentation

Customer segmentation is performed using a Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) to capture overlapping and probabilistic customer behaviors more effectively than rigid clustering methods. By combining RFML features (Recency, Frequency, Monetary, and Loyalty), GMM allows customers to belong to clusters based on likelihood rather than hard boundaries. This approach results in more realistic and interpretable segments that better reflect diverse purchasing patterns within the Olist customer base.

Regression Model

For revenue prediction, a Random Forest regression model is selected due to its ability to model non-linear relationships and handle complex feature interactions. Compared to simpler models, Random Forest demonstrates superior test performance while maintaining robustness against overfitting. This makes it well-suited for predicting total customer revenue and supporting data-driven forecasting and strategic planning.

Tools Used

The project leverages a modern data science toolset to support the full analytical lifecycle. Google Colab is used for data processing, modeling, and experimentation in Python, while Tableau is utilized for interactive dashboards and business-focused visual storytelling. Additionally, Streamlit is prepared for model deployment and result demonstration, enabling stakeholders to interact with predictive outputs in a user-friendly environment.

RFML Distribution

Recency

Most customers have not purchased very recently, the distribution is relatively uniform across 100-400 days, with fewer highly recent customers.

Frequency

The majority of customers are one-time buyers, with a steep drop-off beyond 2 purchases, while a tiny fraction are repeat buyers with significantly higher frequency. This indicates a high acquisition rate but low retention.

Monetary

Most transactions fall below 500 units, with a long tail of high spenders. These rare, high-value customers are likely key revenue drivers.

Loyalty

Most customers have short engagement lifetimes, with most buyers appearing only once or twice across the dataset timeframe while a minority maintain long-term relationships with the platform.
